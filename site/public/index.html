✻Claustrophobic

for when the walls close in, switch rooms.

A multi-account harness for Claude Code. Run several Claude subscriptions side by side: one keystroke launches on the account with the most air left, and your sessions, settings, skills and plugins follow you across accounts — switching mid-task is just exit, cr, keep going.

Install

$ curl -fsSL https://claustrophobic.xyz/install | bash

Setup registers your current ~/.claude login as room 1, installs the c / cr / cw / cl bindings (existing aliases are commented out, with a backup), offers the statusline, and walks you through adding more accounts via the official claude login. Safe to re-run. Prefer to read first? The whole thing is one bash script.

How it works

  1. Every account gets its own room. Extra logins live in isolated CLAUDE_CONFIG_DIR profiles under ~/.claustrophobic/profiles/ — credentials never touch each other, and the official Claude CLI does the logging in.
  2. The rooms share furniture. Sessions, memory, settings.json, skills, agents and plugins are symlinked back to your main ~/.claude, so every room feels identical and cr resumes any session from any account.
  3. The statusline is the sensor. Each render snapshots that account's rate-limit usage; cl and the auto-picker read the snapshots. Idle rooms show their last known air, rooms past their reset count as fresh.

Fine print

Needs macOS or Linux, bash 3.2+, the claude CLI, and python3 or node. Accounts must be distinct emails. Launch flags default to --dangerously-skip-permissions — set during setup, change any time in ~/.claustrophobic/config.

Whether running multiple subscription accounts is within Anthropic's consumer terms is between you and Anthropic — read them before buying three Max plans.

Uninstall: remove the claustrophobic block from your rc file, restore the commented aliases, run claustrophobic statusline uninstall, delete ~/.claustrophobic/.
